Chain-aware sleeve sizing with a 40% per-chain signal cap produced results
virtually identical to the baseline (11.37% CAGR, 6.33 Sharpe, -0.70% max DD).

The cap is not binding for the current 58-vault hand-curated universe. The raw
signal distribution already balances across chains: HyperEVM 20.2%, Arbitrum
20.1%, Base 20.0%, Ethereum 16.3%, Avalanche 14.9%, Monad 8.4% — all well
below 40%.

Despite balanced signal weights, realised PnL remains concentrated in Arbitrum
(48.5%), Base (27.5%), and Ethereum (23.2%). HyperEVM and Avalanche together
contributed less than 1%. This is a vault-quality problem, not a signal-weighting
problem.

Success criteria NOT fully met: chain contribution did not become more balanced
in PnL terms, Sharpe is similar (6.31 vs 6.33), and the mechanism is a no-op
for this universe. However, the mechanism is mechanically correct and would
provide insurance against future universe composition changes.

Conclusive negative for the hypothesis that explicit chain caps improve
diversification in the current universe.
